Billie (born March-ish 2021) is an Australian Cattle Rat ((link)Australian Cattle dog/ (link) Rat Terrier mix.) Also referred to as a good gorl, Billie is a domesticated descendent of the (link)wolf.

Early Life

Billie was a (link)street dog of Oklahoma after escaping from a farm. She was found by a man driving home to Boulder, Colorado on November 18, 2021. She was found without a collar or a chip, but she made herself comfy in the car and insisted on going home with him. Upon her arrival in Boulder, it was clear she wasn’t going to be a good fit with her finder’s dog. This is when her chosen human came into her life, rescued her, named her Billie after (link)Billie Holliday, fashioned her in a (link)Pendleton dog collar, and brought her to her forever home.

Hobbies & Interests

Billie’s DNA encourages herding instincts. Billie loves herding people, dogs, other animals, and her toys. She loves playing fetch, doin’ a (link)snooze, and zooming around. Billie is interested in food of all sorts, but especially red meats.

Diet

Billie’s diet consists of (link)Fromm Family Gold dog food, tastefully prepared with a balanced blend of select proteins and wholesome ingredients for adult dogs. Billie indulges in table food when it misses a human’s mouth, her favorite treat. She loves carrots and (link)Dentley dental chews.

Daily Routines

Billie wakes up when her human wakes up, sometimes that’s 6 am and sometimes that’s 11 am. She does poopoos and peepees every day. After eating her breakfast, she does a zoom around the house with her toys for a while. When her human leaves the house, she does some more snoozin’ in her crate. Billie goes on walks, chases balls, goes on a hike with (link)Sunshine Canyon Dog Rescue every Wednesday, eats dinner, cuddles humans, and barks incessantly at any and every sound that exists.
